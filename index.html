SAINT BENEDICT OF NURSIA

Personal Information

Saint Benedict of Nursia (c. 480 – c. 547) was a Christian monk and the founder of the Benedictine order. He was born in Nursia, Italy, to a noble family and received a strong education in Rome. However, feeling disillusioned with the city life, he withdrew to a cave in Subiaco to live as a hermit. During his time there, his holiness and wisdom drew many followers, and he began establishing monasteries.

Love of Work

His motto, “Ora et Labora” (Pray and Work), showed that both prayer and work are ways to praise God. He encouraged his monks to do daily tasks like farming, cleaning, and teaching with love and care.

Mission

Saint Benedict of Nursia carried out his mission primarily in Italy. He established a community of monks at Subiaco, where he founded twelve monasteries. Later, he moved to Monte Cassino, where he founded the famous monastery that became the birthplace of the Benedictine Order.

Connection

Saint Benedict of Nursia’s significance to Marcellin College is because before Marcellin was a co-educational school in 1982, it was a former all-girls school named after him — St. Benedict College.

Birthday

Saint Benedict was born on March 2, 480 AD in Nursia, Italy.
